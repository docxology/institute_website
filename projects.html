Projects

Institute projects and ecosystem projects convert Active Inference into public artifacts, tools, research, education, and applied practice.

The PDF distinguishes Institute Projects and Ecosystem Projects, with preparation and measurement workflows for proposing, tracking, and reporting work.

Source coverage: Pages 86-166 and 200-204. See the page-by-page source atlas.

Project Lifecycle

Projects are prepared through a proposal process and measured through reporting updates from the Active Inference ecosystem. The public site organizes the project surface around clear titles, missions, outputs, and source-page references.

Institute Projects

Institute projects include recurring research, education, production, ontology, knowledge engineering, software, symposium, and learning-group efforts. They provide internal continuity and public-facing outputs.

Ecosystem Projects

Ecosystem projects are broader community initiatives, including theoretical, applied, domain-specific, and infrastructure efforts that connect Active Inference to external research and practice.
