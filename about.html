About the Institute

A public-interest institution for Active Inference education, research, and applications.

The Active Inference Institute is a participatory Open Science institute dedicated to improving the accessibility, rigor, and applicability of the Active Inference framework.

Source coverage: Pages 11-57. See the page-by-page source atlas.

Purpose

AII supports education, research, training, and applications related to Active Inference. The Institute welcomes all backgrounds, time zones, and familiarity levels, and organizes the ecosystem through programs, projects, public media, research resources, and governance structures.

Mission, Vision, Values, and Principles

The source material emphasizes integrity, open learning, responsible conduct, professional development, excellence in research, and practical service. The public website presents these as an operating stance: make Active Inference more accessible, rigorous, and applicable while supporting ethical collaboration.

Institute Structure

The Institute is represented through a Board of Directors, Officers, Scientific Advisory Board, Members, organizational units, project groups, learning groups, and public communication channels. The structure is meant to support both stable stewardship and flexible participation.

Information Management

The PDF identifies Discord, Coda, newsletters, public video and podcast channels, and project pages as working information surfaces. This website refactors that information into stable public pages while preserving the source atlas for traceability.
