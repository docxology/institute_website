The Foundations of Ideology

 “The Foundations of Ideology”

Research project by Research Fellows @Alexander Hemming


 


Abstract
The Foundations of Ideology develops a novel computational neuroscience framework for understanding how
political ideologies form, persist, and evolve. Grounded in the Free Energy Principle and Active Inference
Framework, this project explains ideology through the brain's fundamental imperative to minimise prediction
error.

Working with Dr. Dylan Grove, we demonstrate how prediction error minimisation strategies shape political belief
formation and updating. Our research identifies how cognitive rigidity, what we define as heuristic adherence (a
lack of nuance surrounding a heuristic persisting through orders of thought), and category conflation constrain
the development of adaptive, accurate political models.

The book that aims to bring these concepts to a general access reader through insights from political theory
(Hannah Arendt, Michael Freeden), political psychology (Bob Altemeyer, John Jost, Philip Tetlock), political
neuroscience science (Leor Zmigrod), and Predictive Coding research, offering both theoretical depth and
practical applications. Using reference to modern political science we further show how ideological polarisation
emerges from fundamental cognitive processes, and identify pathways toward greater integrative complexity in
political thinking.

This framework enables testable predictions about belief formation, offers mechanistic explanations for
polarization, and suggests interventions to promote cognitive flexibility and constructive political discourse.
