• Quantify threshold stability: resistance to overcorrection following single perturbations or rupture events.
• Measure recalibration lag under evidence reversal (e.g. supportive → misleading blocks).
• Identify dynamical profiles of perspective (rigid, hyper-reactive, disorganized, integrative) as regions in
plasticity-stability space.

Environment.
Phase 2 continues to use Phase 1-like Grid world-style environments that efficiently deliver single-agent level
calibration, including Partial observability, Delayed outcome structure, and Misleading local regularities. Training
(formation stage) occurs in encounter-rich ecologies with controlled exposure schedules. Evaluation (assay
stage) uses standardized evidence sequences (supportive, misleading, mixed, reversal) applied identically across
agents.

Deliverables (Month 12).
• Coherence & Threshold Dynamics Metric Suite (v1.0): plasticity, stability, recalibration lag, overcorrection
indices
• Technical Report: evidence for perspective as a robust yet revisable threshold organizer, characterization of
maturity as stability-plasticity coordination.
• (to date 03/27/2026) Working towards preprint(s) to be submitted to the 2026 Artificial Life Conference and
Simulation of Adaptive Behavior 2026.


Months 13-18 / Early Phase 3: Multi-Agent Resonance & Social Attunement
Phase 3 (Early): Multi-Agent Resonance

Core Work.
• Extend architecture to multi-agent settings with independently evolving perspective latents.
• Implement environments where agents must interact under partial observability of others’ latent states.
• Introduce tasks requiring implicit modeling of other agents’ perspectives (no explicit communication channel
required).
• Enable analysis of cross-agent coupling between latent dynamics and behavior.

Analyses.
• Detect regimes of resonance, divergence, drift, and partial alignment between agents.
• Measure cross-agent predictability: how well one agent’s behavior can be anticipated from another’s history.
• Evaluate mutual calibration dynamics: whether agents adjust their thresholds in response to others.
• Analyze stability of interaction patterns under perturbations (e.g. single agent’s perspective-stance shift).
• Test whether interaction outcomes depend on latent perspective compatibility, not just policy similarity.

Environment.
Phase 3 introduces paired or small-group agent settings with observable actions but hidden internal
perspectives, shared environments with partially overlapping goals or constraints, interaction structures that
require agents to interpret consistency, unreliability, or shifts in others’ behavior.
Early setups may remain minimal (gridworld-based), with controlled interaction protocols before scaling to richer
embodied systems.

Deliverables (Month 18).
• Affective Coherence & Integrative Capacity Metric Suite (v1.0): validated measures for affective and integrative
stability.
